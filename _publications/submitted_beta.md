excerpt: 'Here, we use sequence-defined peptoids as programmable scaffolds to engineer azobenzene-based MOST materials with tuneable energy storage and heat release properties. By varying the peptoid backbone length, sequence, and chromophore positioning, we establish structure–property relationships that control storage enthalpy, thermal lifetime, and release kinetics. Peptoids bearing multiple contiguous azobenzenes display cooperative effects, including a 40% enhancement in stored energy, and enable two-step energy release combining rapid heating with sustained thermal output.'
