excerpt: 'This study explores ways to improve phenylazothiazole-based chromophores for molecular solar thermal energy storage by increasing visible-light absorption and stabilizing the energy-rich Z-isomer. It has been shown that attaching the chromophore to a peptidoid backbone extends the lifetime of the Z isomer, especially when positioned at the C-terminus. The work highlights chromophore placement, peptoid sequence design, and solvent choice as key factors for optimizing future MOST materials.<br> **This publication is part of the themed collection [Journal of Materials Chemistry C HOT Papers](https://pubs.rsc.org/en/journals/articlecollectionlanding?sercode=tc&themeid=dbeacc26-6f90-4a7c-851d-c02583f3b81d) !**'
